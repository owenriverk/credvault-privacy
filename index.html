CredVault Privacy Policy

Your credentials, your data, your control.

Effective date: March 28, 2026

CredVault ("the App") is a personal credential and training record manager. This Privacy Policy explains what information the App handles, where it is stored, and the choices available to you. We are committed to respecting your privacy and keeping you in control of your own data.

1. Information We Collect

CredVault stores only the data you explicitly enter or attach. This may include:

  • Credential names and descriptions
  • Issuing bodies and organizations
  • Issue dates, expiration dates, and renewal dates
  • Training records and continuing-education logs
  • Photos and images of credentials or training certificates

We do not collect any information beyond what you voluntarily provide within the App.

2. Where Your Data Is Stored

All data you enter into CredVault stays under your control:

  • On mobile devices — data is stored locally in a SQLite database on your device.
  • On the web — data is stored locally in your browser's built-in storage.
  • Google Drive sync (optional) — if you enable cloud sync, your data is stored in your own personal Google Drive account, in a dedicated CredVault app folder. Only you have access to this folder.

CredVault does not operate any proprietary servers. Your data never passes through or resides on infrastructure controlled by the CredVault team.

3. What We Do NOT Do

  • We do not store your data on any external server.
  • We do not share your data with third parties.
  • We do not collect analytics, usage metrics, or telemetry.
  • We do not display advertisements or use advertising trackers.
  • We do not sell, rent, or monetize your personal data in any way.

4. Google Authentication and Drive Access

CredVault uses Google OAuth 2.0 solely for two purposes:

  • Authentication — to verify your identity so you can access your synced data across devices.
  • Google Drive access — to read from and write to a dedicated CredVault folder within your own Google Drive.

We never see, store, or have access to your Google password. Authentication is handled entirely by Google's secure sign-in flow. The App requests only the minimum permissions necessary to manage the CredVault folder in your Drive.

5. Notifications

CredVault can send you push notifications to remind you when a credential is about to expire. These notifications are scheduled and delivered entirely on your device. No remote push notification service or external server is involved. You can enable or disable notifications at any time from the App's settings or from your device's system settings.

6. Camera and Photo Library Access

The App may request access to your camera or photo library so you can photograph or attach images of your credentials and training certificates. These images are stored only on your device and, if you enable sync, in your Google Drive. They are never uploaded to any other service.

7. Data Deletion

You are in full control of your data and can delete it at any time:

  • From the App — delete individual credentials, training entries, or all data from within the App's interface.
  • From Google Drive — navigate to the CredVault folder in your Google Drive and delete any or all synced files directly.
  • Uninstall — removing the App from your device deletes all locally stored data.

Because we do not maintain any server-side copies of your data, deletion is immediate and permanent.

8. Children's Privacy

CredVault is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
